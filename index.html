The 3-Tier Inventory Framework

Categorizing assets to apply a bespoke sales strategy to each.

🏆 Tier 1: The High-Value Heroes (> €500)

The crown jewels of the inventory. Complex, high-demand items requiring a consultative sales approach. The philosophy is to maximize profit per transaction.

Pricing Rationale

  • **Fixed Price ("Buy It Now"):** Establishes strong perceived value.
  • **"Best Offer" Enabled:** Opens a direct line for negotiation.
  • **Auto-Decline:** Set at 70% of asking price to filter lowball offers.

Risks & Mitigation

  • **Risk:** Higher chance of complex return claims.
  • **Mitigation:** Document serial numbers, use tamper-evident stickers, and always ship with tracking and insurance.

⚡ Tier 2: The Mid-Tier Movers (€50 - €500)

The workhorses of your inventory. Common components with consistent demand. The philosophy is to balance profit with sales velocity.

Pricing Rationale

  • **Competitive Fixed Price:** Price at or just below market average.
  • **Volume Discounts:** Encourage larger orders (e.g., "Buy 2, save 10%").

Risks & Mitigation

  • **Risk:** Competition driving prices down.
  • **Mitigation:** Differentiate with superior listings (photos, details) and excellent service.

📦 Tier 3: The High-Volume Velocity (< €50)

Low-cost items with high quantities. The philosophy is to maximize efficiency and move volume with minimal time per listing.

Pricing Rationale

  • **Sell in Lots/Bundles:** Never sell individually. Bundle into attractive quantities.
  • **Calculated Shipping:** Do not offer free shipping; it will erase profit.

Risks & Mitigation

  • **Risk:** Items sitting if lot size or price is wrong.
  • **Mitigation:** Be flexible. Revise lot sizes or use auctions to test demand.

  • ✓ Bright, diffused light
  • ✓ Minimum 6-8 photos
  • ✓ Clear "Hero Shot"
  • ✓ Close-up of manufacturer's label
  • ✓ Multiple angles (top, bottom, sides)

  • ✓ Included accessories
  • ✓ Any flaws or damage

Advanced Sales & Marketing Playbook

Tools and tactics to maximize visibility and drive sales.

Mastering eBay's Promotional Tools

  • Promoted Listings Standard: Use on all Tier 1 & 2 items. Start with a 5-8% ad rate. Low-risk and essential for visibility.
  • Order Discounts: Encourage larger purchases (e.g., "Save 10% on orders over €200").
  • Markdown Sales: Run weekend sales on specific brands to create urgency and clear stock.

Shipping & Logistics Excellence

  • Professional Packaging: Do not cut corners. Use new boxes and ample padding to prevent damage.
  • Global Shipping Program (GSP): Enable on ALL listings to open your products to a massive international market with zero extra risk.

World-Class Customer Service

  • Respond Quickly: Answer all questions within 12 hours.
  • Be Professional: Use a polite and helpful tone in all communications.
  • Streamline Returns: Offer 30-day returns to build trust. For Tier 1 items, specify that tamper-evident seals must be intact.

The 30-Day Launch Plan

A detailed timeline for a successful first month.

WEEK 1: Foundation & High-Value Focus

  • Finalize inventory categorization into 3 Tiers.
  • Photograph and write detailed descriptions for ALL Tier 1 items.
  • List all Tier 1 items with "Best Offer" and "Promoted Listings".

WEEK 2: Build Momentum & List Core Inventory

  • List at least 15-20 Tier 2 items (Siemens, Rockwell, etc.).
  • Set up your first Order Discount promotion.
  • Respond to offers and analyze initial listing views.

WEEK 3: Efficiency, Bundling & Optimization

  • Identify and bundle Tier 3 items into "lot" listings.
  • Review active listings and revise underperforming titles/photos.
  • List another 15-20 Tier 2 items.

WEEK 4: Promotion, Analysis & Refinement

  • Launch a weekend "Markdown Sale" on a specific brand.
  • Fulfill all orders promptly and professionally.
  • Analyze first month's performance against KPIs to refine strategy.
